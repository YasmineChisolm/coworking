Ethel's club opening means diversity to NYC

By Yasmine Chisolm
September 25, 2019

As of 2019, 67% of New York’s population identifies as a person of color. And while there are hundreds of co-working spaces in NYC there was a lack of diversity, until Ethel's Club - the first co-working and wellness space for people of color that recently opened up in Brooklyn.The 28 year old founder, Naj Austin says that what inspired her to create this space was the lack of diversity. She herself applied to trendy all women’s co-working space the wing, and for 2 years she kept her ear to the ground to see if someone would open a space and when they didn’t she did. The club, which opened its doors to its first 150 members last Monday, has been highly anticipated due to the lack of diversity and it’s strong social media presence and currently has a 4,300-person waitlist.
